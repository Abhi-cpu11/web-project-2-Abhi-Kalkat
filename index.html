What is Treaty One?

Treaty No. 1, signed on August 3, 1871, at Lower Fort Garry in Manitoba, was the first of the Numbered Treaties between the Canadian government and Indigenous peoples. This treaty involved the Anishinaabe and Swampy Cree nations of southern Manitoba. The agreement outlined the cession of specific lands to the Crown in exchange for various commitments, including the distribution of reserve lands, payments, and other benefits to the Indigenous parties.
